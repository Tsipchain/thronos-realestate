# Missions

Missions introduce the core mechanics of the Crypto Hunters game.  They are
divided into four tiers of difficulty: Easy, Normal, Expert and Genius.

## Easy Missions

* **E1 – Hello Hash**
  - Objective: Compute simple SHA‑256 hashes.
  - Reward: 100 DRX.

* **E2 – Nonce Nudge**
  - Objective: Find a nonce that yields a hash below a small target.
  - Reward: 100 DRX.

* **E3 – Block Anatomy**
  - Objective: Assemble block header fields in their correct order.
  - Reward: 100 DRX.

## Normal Missions

* **N1 – Merkle Scout**
  - Objective: Rebuild a Merkle root from supplied leaf hashes.
  - Reward: 250 DRX.

* **N2 – Auth Check**
  - Objective: Implement HMAC verification for a message.
  - Reward: 250 DRX.

* **N3 – Replay Guard**
  - Objective: Detect and block replay attempts in an API context.
  - Reward: 250 DRX.

## Expert Missions

* **X1 – TX Validator**
  - Objective: Simulate transaction validation rules of a decentralised ledger.
  - Reward: 500 DRX.

* **X2 – Protocol Patch**
  - Objective: Patch a vulnerable routine in a consensus protocol.
  - Reward: 500 DRX.

* **X3 – Rate‑Limit Forge**
  - Objective: Tune anti‑bot rate limits to prevent denial of service.
  - Reward: 500 DRX.

## Genius Missions

* **G1 – Anomaly Hunt**
  - Objective: Detect threats hidden in noisy telemetry streams.
  - Reward: 1,000 DRX.

* **G2 – Consensus Siege**
  - Objective: Defend the consensus mechanism while under coordinated attack.
  - Reward: 1,000 DRX.

* **G3 – Zero‑Day Drill**
  - Objective: Respond to a zero‑day exploit affecting multiple shards.
  - Reward: 1,000 DRX.

# Crypto Hunters: Story Bible

## 1. Logline

In a world reeling from financial crisis, a small team of renegade technologists—The Crypto Hunters—must defend the fragile dream of a decentralised future from the Dark Node, a shadowy cabal determined to maintain control at any cost.

## 2. Expanded Premise

The year is 2012. As Greece buckles under the weight of austerity, a brilliant but disillusioned hacker named **Stavros Athanasios Fakinos**, known by his handle **SAfA**, stumbles upon a whitepaper by Satoshi Nakamoto. He sees not just a digital currency, but a blueprint for a new world—one free from the corruptible influence of centralised power. His journey through the volatile early days of crypto, marked by euphoric highs and devastating crashes, forges his idealism into a hardened resolve. Realising this new paradigm is under threat from the very forces it seeks to replace, he assembles a team of specialists: The Crypto Hunters. Operating from the shadows, they are the guardians of the blockchain, the protectors of the protocol, and the only thing standing between a decentralised tomorrow and a future of absolute control.

## 4. Factions & Alliances

-   **The Crypto Hunters:** Our heroes. A small, agile unit dedicated to proactive defence, education, and building secure tools for the decentralised world. Their base of operations is "The Source," a hidden, self-sufficient server farm and workshop beneath a forgotten library in Athens.
-   **The White Hat Collective:** A loose global network of ethical hackers and security researchers whom the Hunters can call upon for support. They share intel and collaborate on defending critical infrastructure but are not a formal organisation.
-   **The Agora Project:** A forward-thinking branch of the Greek government, experimenting with a decentralised civic stack for voting, property registration, and public finance. They are the Hunters' first and most important "client."

## 5. World & Locations

-   **The Source (Athens, Greece):** The Hunters' HQ. A mix of high-tech servers, 3D printers, and soldering stations set against ancient stone walls. The air hums with the sound of fans and the smell of ozone and old paper.
-   **The Forge (Piraeus, Greece):** Nato Saka’s domain. A sprawling, disused shipyard warehouse where he stress-tests hardware, builds custom nodes, and hosts a "community node" farm powered by solar and ingenuity. It’s a chaotic symphony of metal, wires, and raw power.
-   **The Relay (Gibraltar):** A primary command-and-control server for the Dark Node, hidden within a legitimate-looking, high-security data centre. It’s a key target for intelligence gathering.
-   **The Exchange (Off-shore):** A popular but secretly compromised cryptocurrency exchange used by the Dark Node to launder funds, manipulate markets, and harvest user data. It represents the corruptible side of the ecosystem.
-   **Digital Arenas:** The true battlefields. These are not physical places but visual representations of networks under attack: consensus mechanisms visualised as shifting geometric structures, data packets as streams of light, and malicious code as jagged, crystalline corruption.

## 6. Lore & Terminology

-   **The Genesis Block:** The term used by SAfA and the team to refer to the moment they first understood the potential of blockchain. It's their personal "Year Zero."
-   **Proof-of-Work:** In-game, this is often represented as a Herculean task—a mission that requires immense resources (computational or otherwise) to solve a single, critical problem that secures the network for everyone.
-   **51% Attack:** The Dark Node's ultimate weapon. A coordinated, brute-force assault to seize control of a network. In the story, this is treated like a digital nuclear option, a doomsday scenario the Hunters must prevent at all costs.
-   **The Civic Stack:** The suite of decentralised applications being piloted by the Greek government. Its success or failure will determine the future of decentralisation in the public sphere.

## 7. Plot Outline (Expanded Acts)

**Act 1: Spark (2012-2013)**
-   **Opening:** SAfA, disillusioned by the Greek debt crisis, discovers the Bitcoin whitepaper. He sets up a rudimentary mining operation in his apartment.
-   **First Contact:** He successfully mines his first block. The feeling is euphoric, a taste of true financial sovereignty.
-   **The Bull Run:** The 2013 bull run makes SAfA a small fortune. He sees it as validation, believing the revolution is already here. He invests heavily in new altcoins and exchanges like Mt. Gox.
-   **The Crash:** The Mt. Gox collapse wipes him out. It's a brutal lesson: decentralisation is meaningless without security. Devastated, he seeks out his old friend Nato Saka, a hardware genius, realising the fight must be waged on both software and hardware fronts.

**Act 2: Formation (2014-2016)**
-   **The First Exploit:** The Dark Node, in its infancy, orchestrates a successful 51% attack on a promising altcoin, causing chaos and discrediting the project.
-   **Assembly:** The attack draws the attention of others. **Niva**, investigating the financial fallout for her agency, sees a pattern the authorities are ignoring. **Kyra**, an on-chain analyst, traces the ghost funds back to a network of sophisticated actors. **Luna**, a core developer for the targeted coin, is trying to rally the community to perform a hard fork. SAfA and Nato find them, and the five unite, realising they are all fighting the same shadow war. The Crypto Hunters are born.
-   **Early Victories:** The team thwarts several attacks: they prevent a phishing campaign targeting a major wallet service, expose a vulnerability in an early smart contract platform, and secure a network of community nodes.

**Act 3: Revelation (Mid-Season)**
-   **A New Threat:** The Dark Node's attacks become more sophisticated, moving beyond simple financial gain to targeted disinformation campaigns. They are not just trying to steal; they are trying to destroy the idea of trust in the system.
-   **The Ghost in the Machine:** The Hunters hit a wall. The enemy is too well-funded, too coordinated. Out of the blue, they receive an encrypted message from an unknown source: **Zayne Nexus**.
-   **The Intelligence:** Zayne provides them with a partial schematic of the Dark Node's infrastructure and reveals their true nature: a global conspiracy to co-opt or crush decentralisation. He warns them that their next target is not a coin, but a country: the Greek government's "Agora Project."

**Act 4: Uplift (Season Finale)**
-   **The Siege of Agora:** The Dark Node launches a multi-pronged assault to destroy the Agora Project and prove that decentralised governance is a failure.
    -   **Digital Front:** A massive DDoS attack combined with an attempt to exploit a vulnerability in the voting protocol's smart contract.
    -   **Social Front:** A state-sponsored media FUD campaign to turn the Greek public against the project.
    -   **Physical Front:** An agent is dispatched to sabotage the core server hardware hosted in a government facility.
-   **Climactic Showdown:** The Hunters must split up to defend all three fronts simultaneously.
    -   SAfA and Luna race to patch the code live.
    -   Kyra and Zayne (remotely) work to counter the FUD with on-chain proof of the system's integrity.
    -   Niva and Nato must physically intercept the saboteur before they can reach the server room.
-   **Victory and a Warning:** They succeed, and the Agora Project pilot is a resounding success, making international headlines. In his final message, Zayne congratulates them but warns them: "You've cut off the head of a hydra. The Dark Node in Greece was just one of them." The season ends with the team looking at a world map, realising their fight has just gone global.
